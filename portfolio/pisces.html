PISCES

Project Overview

>> The Planetary In-Situ Capillary Electrophoresis System (PISCES) is a portable, miniature, modular instrument designed to analyze molecular signatures of extraterrestrial life. The instrument uses microcapillary electrophoresis (µCE) with laser-induced fluorescence detection (LIFD) to analyze fluorescently-labeled analytes prepared on a multilayer microfluidic chip.
>> PISCES is divided into three main systems:
[1] The pneumatic system is the processing core of PISCES. It contains 48 electrofluidic solenoid valves which open and close together in various sequences to induce movement of fluids through an array of interconnecting channels on one layer of the microfluidic chip. On this layer of the chip, introduced samples are processed with a set of stored reagents before separation by ÂµCE.
[2] The µCE system is the analytical core of PISCES. It contains an electrophoretic channel integrated into a separate layer of the microfluidic chip. By generating a strong electric field across the fluidic medium in the channel, separation of a chemical mixture into components of differing mass and charge is achieved.
[3] The LIFD system is the detection core of PISCES. It uses an optical pathway and spectrometer to detect laser induced fluorescence of labeled analytes passing through the electrophoretic channel.
>> These systems are divided into five modules, each of which can be easily detached from the system for subsystem testing in extreme environments mimicking the desired planetary target. The modules and their associated responsibilities are as follows:
[1] Pneumatic Module: contains pneumatics and control circuitry required to operate the 48 electro-fludic solenoid valves responsible for on-chip sample processing.
[2] High-voltage Module: contains circuitry required to apply electrical potentials between 3 - 15 kV to the microdevice's electrophoretic channel.
[3] Optical Electronics Module: contains the spectrometer, laser, and laser power supply necessary for LIFD.
[4] Optics Module: contains the optical pathway directing laser light to the chip, and fluorescent light to the spectrometer.
[5] Power Supply Module: contains batteries and circuitry required to power the above modules.
