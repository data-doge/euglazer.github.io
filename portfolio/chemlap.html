Background

The Chemical Laptop, co-engineered with Los Gatos Research and HJ Science + Technology, is a portable, miniaturized instrument designed to detect molecular signatures of life in liquid samples. Operated by a tablet computer, it uses microcapillary electrophoresis (µCE) coupled with laser-induced fluorescence detection (LIFD) to analyze labeled samples pneumatically prepared and transported on a multilayer microfluidic chip. The primary objective of my work that summer was to test and characterize the newly developed Chemical Laptop prototype, and prepare the instrument for a completely automated, end-to-end field analysis. This required modifications to its existing hardware and software, and the development of new hardware and software.

Partners

Peter Willis, Amanda Stockton, Maria Mora, Morgan Cable, Los Gatos Research, HJ Science + Technology

My Responsibilities

1. Revised software to reduce complexity of spectral data and optimize automated sample processing cycles.
2. Wrote software allowing for automated, on-chip colorimetric determination and adjustment of sample pH.
3. Explored a variety of fabrication methods, materials, and post-fabrication procedures to develop a lightweight, air-tight, pneumatic manifold resistant to deformation under high pressure.
4. Designed a simple mounting system and installed a USB microscope for automated colorimetric pH control and remote observation.
5. Prototyped, miniature, modular, refillable cartridges to dispense all necessary reagents required for the analysis of numerous compound classes by a simple clamping and switching mechanism.
